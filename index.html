Abstract

Flood hazard mapping with minimal resources

Flood hazard mapping is essential for disaster prevention but remains challenging in data-scarce regions, where traditional hydrodynamic models require extensive geophysical inputs. ZeroFlood leverages geospatial foundation models to predict flood hazard maps from single-modality Earth-observation data, specifically SAR imagery.

We construct a dataset that pairs EO data with flood hazard simulations across the European continent and evaluate recent GeoFMs for flood hazard segmentation. TerraMind reaches an F1-score of 88.36%, exceeding supervised baselines by more than 3 percentage points, and Thinking-in-Modality improves performance further by compensating for missing spatial information such as LULC and DEM.

Method

The ZeroFlood pipeline

A

Pair EO with flood simulations

TerraMesh modalities are spatially aligned with LISFLOOD-FP hazard simulations to create training targets.

B

Train GeoFM flood decoders

GeoFM encoders are frozen and connected to a UPerNet decoder for flood hazard segmentation.

C

Recover missing modalities

TiM generates auxiliary tokens, for example DEM or LULC, from the available EO modality.

D

Deploy in sparse-data regions

The trained model can infer hazard masks from SAR imagery where hydrodynamic inputs are unavailable.

Results

GeoFMs improve flood hazard segmentation

The paper reports mIoU, F1, recall/HitRate, and precision/TrueAlarm. TerraMind is the strongest base GeoFM, and TiM variants often improve recall by injecting generated modality tokens.

  • SAR-only prediction is viable: TerraMind reaches 88.36 F1 from S1RTC alone, beating UNet by 3.70 points.
  • TiM helps the single-modality case: TerraMind-TiM-l improves S1RTC to 89.12 F1 and lifts recall to 88.45.
  • Optical input is also strong: S2L2A with TerraMind reaches 88.58 F1 with the highest S2 precision, 90.60.
  • Two modalities set the best ceiling: S1RTC + S2L2A reaches 89.16 F1 and 91.25 precision with TerraMind.
